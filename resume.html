Projects

Torpor:

An experimental narrative game where the player pieces together the story of a soldier through a fragmented series of vignettes surrounding his life. I made this game with an artist friend in two weeks for Adventure Jam. It was published on Itch.io and Gamejolt, and was covered by RockPaperShotgun, PCGamer, AlphaBetaGamer, and Markiplier.

Engine: Unity

Team size: 2

Contribution: Design, Writing, Code.

Don't Drop Your Nuts:

A small minigame in which players catch nuts that fall from the sky. Made at an overnight game jam with the University of Utah Gamecraft club. I lead a team of programmers in designing and implementing the code while communicating with the art team to push towards a finished product.

Engine: Unity

Team size: 19

Contribution: Lead Engineer

Retrostasis:

Open-world 2D puzzle platformer in which players have to strategically conserve energy to get through puzzles and fix their robot friend. I made all of the art, levels, and code in 72 hours for Gamejolt's GDC jam. Retrostasis won 4th place out of 120 games, and I showed it at GDC.

Engine: Unity

Contribution: Sole developer

C Interpreter:

As a side project I made a simple lexer, parser, and interpreter for C-like code implemented in C#. The goal was to interpret code at runtime in Unity that could interface with a game's world. It's currently still in a very early stage. Check it out on github.

Contribution: Sole developer
