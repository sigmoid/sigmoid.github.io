about me

I'm a SLC based game programmer and student of Computer Science.

Developing games started as a hobby when I was in middle school, and quickly became my singular passion. I've spent countless nights and weekends making games, and it's paid off! Games I've worked on have been featured on PCGamer, received awards and praise, and been played by tens of thousands of people (including Markiplier!).

Retrostasis

A 2D puzzle platformer where players must conserve energy and remember how to get back to their friend.

I made the original prototype of Retrostasis solo in 72 hours for Gamejolt's GDC jam. Afterwards I developed the game further with a team of 4 over the course of a summer.


One of 5 winners out of 128 entries in GDC jam 2016.

Showcased at Gamejolt's booth GDC 2016.

Nominated Game of the Year Gamejolt Jolties award.

Please Respond, Colony One

Paint landscapes and discover the memories of the last survivor of the first martian colony.

I'm programming all of the core gameplay systems, shaders, and UI. At the core of the game is an animated post-process shader that implements the Kuwahara filter on the GPU.
